# Vision

Create a simple and motivating reading tracker iOS app that helps users stay consistent with their yearly reading goals without overwhelming them. The app focuses on clarity, ease of use, and lightweight interaction rather than deep analytics or complex features.

# Audience
- Casual readers who want to build a reading habit
- Users who prefer simple tools over feature-heavy apps
- People setting yearly reading goals (e.g., “read 12 books this year”)
- Mobile-first users who value quick input and minimal friction

# Success Criteria
- Users can add and manage books in under a few seconds
- High completion rate of adding books (low drop-off)
- Users consistently update book status (engagement)
- Clear visibility of progress toward yearly goal
- No confusion or errors during core flows (add, edit, update status)

# Product Decisions
- Simplicity over complexity
-The app avoids advanced features like recommendations, social sharing, or detailed analytics. -The goal is to keep the experience focused and frictionless.

# “Quick thoughts” instead of full reviews
Users are encouraged to write short impressions (max 500 characters) rather than detailed reviews. This reduces effort and increases completion rate.

# Limited book count (1–100)
A cap ensures the app stays performant and manageable, while still covering realistic yearly goals.

# Status-based tracking
Books are tracked using simple states: "not started", "in progress", and "finished". This provides clarity without overcomplicating progress tracking.

# Goal-driven experience
The yearly reading goal is central to the app, helping users stay motivated and measure progress clearly.

# Prompt instead of disabled actions
When users reach the book limit, a prompt is shown instead of disabling actions. This improves clarity and avoids confusion.
